Inititl draft:
This system and its intended uses are on the surface ethically and socially positive as it allows accessibility to academic research by allowing users to find theses through semantic search rather than exact keyword matching. This makes academic and research material easier to search and access helping students, researchers and the average person. The summarisation feature also helps users understand large documents more quickly by generating short overviews of thesis content.

Although there is a possibility that this model in either its current or future state may  exhibit ethical concerns. The sematic model is pretrained on external models and vectoring systems, this might make the result retrieval biased and favour certain writing styles and differences in terminology. Semantic ranking of the model might also give the impression that the results ranking is a ranking of the most relevant article when it is just the closest semantically.

The AI summary feature in this model also might have ethical implications as while summarising the work, it is presenting the full research and may reduce nuance or context. Important methodological limitations, cautious conclusions or complex arguments may be simplified too heavily when converted into short summaries.OCR processing also introduces an unfair advantage. Older academic research that has not been digitised has to be converted from handwritten to digitised which may not be entirely accurate and make good results less visible simply due to their form of input rather than academic relevance
